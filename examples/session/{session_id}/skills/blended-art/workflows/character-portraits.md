# Character & Persona Portraits Workflow

**Visualizing characters, personas, entities, and agents using Klaus aesthetic and the 2-style organic system.**

Creates **CHARACTER PORTRAITS** â€” single focused portrait with narrative elements using Sumi-e Ink or Blended Hand-Drawn, showing WHO someone/something IS rather than abstract metaphor.

---

## Purpose

Character portraits visualize entities, personas, agents, or characters as recognizable visual subjects. These are **literal portraiture with narrative depth** â€” not abstract metaphor, but actual "this is what this entity looks like."

**Use this workflow for:**
- "Visualize this character"
- "What does this entity look like?"
- "Draw this persona"
- AI agent visualization
- Brand mascot/character design
- Self-portraits (like Klaus)
- Personified concepts (death, time, chaos)
- Team/role archetypes

**Key difference from Editorial Illustration:** Editorial Illustration creates abstract metaphors. Character Portraits create literal visual representations of WHO someone/something IS.

## Visual Aesthetic: 2-Style Organic System for Character Portraits

**Both organic styles work for portraits â€” selection based on character nature:**

### Style Selection

**Sumi-e Ink (For mysterious, zen, ancient characters):**
- Brush stroke expression captures essence
- Negative space suggests depth/mystery
- Ink wash gradients create atmosphere
- Best for: Wise entities, ancient beings, meditative characters, felt presence

**Blended Hand-Drawn (For evolving, authentic, process-oriented characters):**
- Sketch shows character still forming/becoming
- Digital overlay shows what's defined
- Visible construction marks show character complexity
- Best for: AI agents, personas in transition, authentic/vulnerable characters

**Blended Hand-Drawn (For characters in process of becoming):**
- Sketch + digital shows character still forming
- Visible construction shows character's journey
- Organic + geometric tension shows hybrid nature
- Best for: Evolving entities, AI agents, characters between states

### Core Characteristics
1. **Single subject focus** â€” One character/entity as clear subject
2. **Narrative elements** â€” Visual details that tell the character's story
3. **Recognizable identity** â€” Character is visually distinct and memorable
4. **Environmental context** â€” Subtle background hints at character's world
5. **Expression/pose** â€” Communicates character's essential nature
6. **Symbolic attributes** â€” Objects/elements that define the character
7. **Klaus aesthetic** â€” Brand colors integrated into character design

### Step 1: Define the Character

**Identify WHO you're portraying:**

1. **What/Who is this character?**

   - Role or function

2. **What are their defining attributes?**

3. **What's their story?**
   - Where do they come from?
   - What defines their journey?
   - What are they becoming?

### Step 2: Choose Portrait Approach

**Determine how to frame the character:**

1. **Portrait framing:**

   - What emotion or state to capture?
   - Dynamic or static pose?
   - Facing viewer or profile?

3. **Background context:**
   - Pure negative space (isolated character)
   - Subtle environmental hints
   - Full environmental context

### Step 5: Determine Aspect Ratio

| Portrait Type | Aspect Ratio | Reasoning |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Close-up / Face focus | 1:1 | Square for intimate portrait |
| Half-body | 4:5 or 3:4 | Vertical portrait format |
| Full-body | 9:16 | Tall figure format |
| Environmental | 16:9 | Wide to show context |
| Profile/cinematic | 21:9 | Ultra-wide dramatic |

**Default: 1:1 (square)** â€” Classic portrait format, works everywhere
