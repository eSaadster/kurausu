# Emotional Landscape & Feeling Map Workflow

**Mapping emotional journeys, felt states, and affective terrain using Klaus aesthetic and the 2-style organic system.**

Creates **EMOTIONAL LANDSCAPES** â€” flowing, organic visualizations of how something FEELS, using Sumi-e Ink (primary) or Blended Hand-Drawn style.

## Purpose

Emotional landscapes map the FEELING of something â€” not what it IS structurally, but how it FEELS experientially. These are **affective terrain maps** â€” showing emotional journeys, felt states, and inner weather.

**Use this workflow for:**
- "How does this feel?"
- "Map the emotional arc"
- "What's the felt experience?"
- Emotional journey visualization
- User experience feeling maps
- Inner state landscapes
- Affective terrain of a concept
- Mood mapping

**Key differences:**
- **vs Conceptual Maps:** Those map IDEAS territorially, this maps FEELINGS organically
- **vs Frameworks:** Those are structural, this is felt
- **vs Timelines:** Those track time, this tracks emotional shifts

This is about SENSATION, not STRUCTURE.

## Visual Aesthetic: 2-Style Organic System for Emotional Landscapes

**Recommended styles:** Sumi-e Ink (primary) or Blended Hand-Drawn

### Style Selection

**Sumi-e Ink (PRIMARY â€” Perfect for feeling):**
- Brush strokes express emotional intensity
- Ink wash gradients show mood shifts
- MA (é–“) negative space as emotional breathing room
- Organic, flowing, felt

**Blended Hand-Drawn (For complex/layered feelings):**
- Sketch shows raw, unprocessed feeling
- Digital overlay shows recognized/named emotions
- Layers show feeling complexity
- Construction marks show emotions still forming

**Watercolor approach within Sumi-e (For soft transitions):**
- Transparent washes bleed into each other
- Colors mix at emotion boundaries
- Soft, atmospheric, immersive

### Core Characteristics
1. **Organic terrain** â€” Hills, valleys, currents, not boxes or zones
2. **Flow and gradient** â€” Emotions blend, don't have hard edges
3. **Intensity variation** â€” Dense/sparse, high/low, sharp/soft
4. **Embodied metaphor** â€” Feelings as landscape (weight, warmth, texture)
5. **Journey possible** â€” Can trace emotional path through terrain
6. **NOT structural** â€” No boxes, no grids, no frameworks
7. **Felt truth** â€” Represents sensation, not analysis

### Color Strategy
- **Crab Red = Heat** â€” Where feelings are most intense
- **Gold Leaf = Warmth** â€” Transitional, hopeful zones
- **Black = Weight** â€” Heavy, deep emotional territory
- **White/Cream = Breath** â€” Open, calm, spacious
