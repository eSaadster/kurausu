## Style Direction

### The 2 Organic Styles (AI selects based on content)

**1. Sumi-e Ink (Japanese Ink Painting) â€” THE FELT EXPERIENCE**
- Expressive black ink brush strokes with varying thickness
- **Hajime** (beginning) strokes thick and loaded with ink
- **Owari** (ending) strokes taper to fine points
- Extensive **MA (é–“)** â€” intentional negative space (zen emptiness)
- Ink wash gradients from deep black to subtle gray
- Organic, flowing linework (NOT geometric)
- Crab Red as HANKO SEAL ACCENT (artist's stamp style)
- Gold Leaf for subtle premium touches
- Use for: abstract concepts, emotional themes, zen metaphors, philosophical content, feeling maps, narrative flow

**2. Blended Hand-Drawn (Sketch + Digital Fusion) â€” THE VISIBLE PROCESS**
- Rough organic ink sketches as base layer (wavy, variable line weight)
- Clean digital geometric elements overlaid (precise shapes, flat colors)
- **Visible construction marks** â€” working process shown, not hidden
- Intentional "unfinished" quality showing authenticity
- Layers coexist but don't fully merge (sketch visible beneath digital)
- Crab Red as CRISP GEOMETRIC accent layer cutting through organic sketch
- Gold Leaf as BRIDGE between organic and digital worlds
- Negative space balances between deliberate (zen) and organic (sketchbook)
- Use for: personal narratives, process explanations, hybrid concepts, authentic/vulnerable content, "escape from polish" moments, working notes

### Japanese Brush Stroke Terminology (for Sumi-e prompts)
| Term | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Hajime** | Beginning stroke | Thick, loaded with ink |
| **Owari** | Ending stroke | Tapers to fine point |
| **Fude-atari** | Brush touch | Variation in pressure |
| **Ma (é–“)** | Negative space | Intentional emptiness |
| **Notan** | Light/dark balance | Contrast harmony |
