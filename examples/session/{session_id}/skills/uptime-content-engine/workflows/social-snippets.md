## Platform Personalities

### LinkedIn
```
TONE: Professional thought leadership
LENGTH: 150-300 words (posts), 3-5 words (hooks)
AUDIENCE: Fleet managers, HR leaders, operations executives
GOAL: Establish authority, drive engagement, generate leads

WHAT WORKS:
- Industry insights with data
- Contrarian takes on hiring practices
- Personal stories from leadership
- Customer success highlights
- "What I learned" posts

WHAT DOESN'T:
- Direct product pitches
- Generic corporate announcements
- Stock photo carousels
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
```

### Twitter/X
```
TONE: Punchy, provocative, fast
LENGTH: Under 280 characters per tweet
AUDIENCE: Industry professionals, trucking community
GOAL: Brand awareness, engagement, thought leadership

WHAT WORKS:
- Single stats that stop the scroll
- Hot takes on industry
- Quick tips (3 or fewer)
- Responding to industry news
- Thread starters with value

WHAT DOESN'T:
- Long-form content (use threads)
- Corporate speak
- Hashtag stuffing
- Pure promotion without value
```

### Instagram
```
TONE: Visual-first, human, authentic
LENGTH: 125-200 words (captions)
AUDIENCE: Broader trucking community, drivers, industry
GOAL: Brand building, humanizing the company, driver stories

WHAT WORKS:
- Driver spotlights and stories
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Data visualizations
- Quote graphics
- Team and culture content

WHAT DOESN'T:
- Text-heavy posts
- Corporate jargon
- Posts without strong visuals
- Overly salesy content
```

### Step 2: Write the Hook

**The first line determines if anyone reads the rest.**

**Hook formulas:**
```
THE PROVOCATIVE STATEMENT:
"Your ATS is a $50,000/year suggestion box."

THE SURPRISING STAT:
"$1,200. That's what every empty seat costs you. Per day."

THE QUESTION:
"What if everything you know about the 'driver shortage' is wrong?"

THE CONTRARIAN:
"Unpopular opinion: Sign-on bonuses are making your retention worse."

THE STORY STARTER:
"Three years ago, our trucks sat idle for months at a time."

THE DIRECT ADDRESS:
"Fleet managers, we need to talk about your hiring costs."
```

**Hook rules:**
```
- First line stands alone (line break after)
- No filler words ("I wanted to share...")
- Specific > General
- Provoke curiosity or challenge assumption
```

### Step 7: Validate

**Checklist:**

- [ ] **Hook stops scroll?** Would you stop for this?
- [ ] **Platform appropriate?** Tone and length match
- [ ] **Value provided?** Teaches, provokes, or resonates
- [ ] **Not salesy?** Value first, product second (or not at all)
- [ ] **On-brand voice?** Direct, confident, data-backed
- [ ] **Clear CTA?** (if applicable) Obvious next step
- [ ] **Visual attached?** (if applicable) Supports the message
- [ ] **Hashtags appropriate?** (Instagram) Relevant, not spammy

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Weak hook | First line must stop the scroll |
| Direct selling | Value first, product mention secondary |
| Generic corporate voice | Direct, human, specific |
| Wall of text | Line breaks, bullets, short paragraphs |
| No CTA | End with question or clear next step |
| Wrong tone for platform | Match platform culture |
| Hashtag overload | Minimal on LinkedIn/Twitter, strategic on Instagram |
| Engagement bait | Genuine questions, not "Like if you agree" |
