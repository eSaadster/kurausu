# Email Copy Workflow

**Creates email content that gets opened, read, and acted on.**

## Purpose

Email is Uptime's most direct channel. Unlike social, you've already earned their attentionâ€”now you need to keep it. Every email should deliver value, build trust, and move the relationship forward.

## Email Types

### Type 1: Newsletter / Value Email
Regular content delivery.
```
Goal: Deliver value, maintain relationship, build trust
Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
Length: 300-500 words
CTA: Soft (read more, learn more)
```

### Type 2: Nurture Email
Part of automated sequence.
```
Goal: Move prospect through funnel
Frequency: Based on trigger
Length: 200-400 words
CTA: Specific action (demo, trial, call)
```

### Type 3: Announcement Email
Product or company news.
```
Goal: Inform and excite
Frequency: As needed
Length: 200-300 words
CTA: Learn more or try feature
```

### Type 4: Re-engagement Email
Win back inactive contacts.
```
Goal: Re-activate interest
Frequency: Triggered by inactivity
Length: 150-250 words
CTA: Single, clear action
```

### Type 5: Onboarding Email
New user welcome and guidance.
```
Goal: Activate user, show value
Frequency: First 7-14 days
Length: 150-300 words
CTA: Complete setup step
```

### Step 2: Write the Subject Line

**Subject line is everything. 47% of email recipients decide to open based on subject line alone.**

**Formulas:**
```
THE QUESTION:
"What's your real cost-per-hire?"
"Still chasing drivers?"

THE PROVOCATIVE STATEMENT:
"Your ATS is a $50k suggestion box"
"The hiring math no one talks about"

THE PERSONAL:
"Quick question about your hiring"
"[Name], this caught my attention"

THE SPECIFIC VALUE:
"5 interview questions that predict retention"
"The 14-day hiring timeline"

THE URGENCY (use sparingly):
"Your trucks are waiting"
"Before your next hire"
```

**Subject line rules:**
```
- 40 characters max (mobile-friendly)
- No ALL CAPS
- No excessive punctuation (!!!)
- No spam trigger words (FREE, ACT NOW)
- Test with and without personalization
- Preview text matters too (use it)
```

**Preview text (preheader):**
```
- 90-130 characters
- Complements subject line
- Adds context or teases content
- Never repeats subject line

EXAMPLE:
Subject: "What's your real cost-per-hire?"
Preview: "Most calculations miss 60% of the actual cost. Here's what you're not counting."
```

---

### Step 3: Write the Opening

**First sentence earns the second. No wasted words.**

**Opening formulas:**
```
THE DIRECT ADDRESS:
"Your hiring process is taking too long. I'll explain whyâ€”and what to do about it."

THE QUESTION:
"How long did your last driver hire take? 60 days? 90 days? Longer?"

THE STORY:
"Last week, I talked to a fleet manager who'd been trying to fill a seat for 4 months."

THE STAT:
"The average fleet takes 84 days to hire a driver. 84 days of empty seats."

THE ACKNOWLEDGMENT:
"I know your inbox is full of companies claiming they can fix your hiring.
Here's why we're different:"
```

**Opening rules:**
```
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No "I wanted to reach out"
- No "As a leader in..."
- Get to the point in first sentence
- Create immediate relevance
```

### Step 4: Write the Body

**Every sentence must earn its place.**

**Body structure for newsletters:**
```
[HOOK - Why this matters now]

[MAIN CONTENT - The value]
- Insight 1
- Insight 2
- Insight 3

[TAKEAWAY - What to do with this]

[CTA - Soft ask]
```

**Body structure for nurture:**
```
[HOOK - Connect to their situation]

[PROBLEM - The pain they're feeling]

[SOLUTION HINT - What's possible]

[PROOF - Data or example]

[CTA - Clear next step]
```

**Body structure for announcements:**
```
[NEWS - What's new]

[WHY IT MATTERS - For them]

[HOW IT WORKS - Brief]

[CTA - Try it / Learn more]
```

**Formatting rules:**
```
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One idea per paragraph
- Bullet points for lists
- Bold for emphasis (sparingly)
- White space is your friend
```

---

### Step 5: Write the CTA

**One email = one CTA. Maximum two if absolutely necessary.**

**CTA formulas:**
```
THE BUTTON:
[See How It Works]
[Book Your Demo]
[Get Started Free]

THE TEXT LINK:
"Click here to see the full breakdown."
"Ready to see it in action? Book a quick call here."

THE REPLY:
"Reply to this email with 'demo' and I'll send you available times."

THE SOFT:
"Worth exploring? Here's where to start: [link]"
```

**CTA rules:**
```
- One primary CTA per email
- Make the action crystal clear
- Button for marketing emails
- Text link for personal-feeling emails
- Low friction (15-min call > 1-hour meeting)
```

## Email Sequences

### Welcome Sequence (New Subscriber)
```
EMAIL 1 (Day 0): Welcome + Set expectations
Subject: "Welcome to Uptimeâ€”here's what's next"
Content: What they'll get, what to expect, who we are
CTA: Read our most popular post / Explore dashboard

EMAIL 2 (Day 2): First value delivery
Subject: "The math no one talks about in hiring"
Content: Top insight or resource
CTA: Read full article / Download guide

EMAIL 3 (Day 5): Problem agitation
Subject: "How long is too long to hire a driver?"
Content: Industry problem + how Uptime solves it
CTA: See how it works

EMAIL 4 (Day 8): Social proof
Subject: "From 84 days to 14: How they did it"
Content: Customer success story
CTA: Book a demo
```

### Demo Nurture (Post-Demo Request)
```
EMAIL 1 (Immediately): Confirmation
Subject: "You're bookedâ€”here's what to expect"
Content: Confirmation, what to prepare, what you'll see
CTA: Add to calendar

EMAIL 2 (Day before): Reminder + value
Subject: "Tomorrow: Your Uptime demo"
Content: Reminder + one insight to think about
CTA: Prepare a question for us

EMAIL 3 (Day after): Follow-up
Subject: "Following up on your Uptime demo"
Content: Key points covered, next steps
CTA: [Specific next step based on conversation]
```

### Re-engagement Sequence (30+ Days Inactive)
```
EMAIL 1 (Day 30): Soft check-in
Subject: "Still looking for drivers?"
Content: Brief, casual check-in
CTA: Let us know what's going on

EMAIL 2 (Day 37): New value
Subject: "Something new you might have missed"
Content: Recent content/feature they'd find valuable
CTA: Check it out

EMAIL 3 (Day 45): Direct ask
Subject: "Should I close your file?"
Content: Clear, honest ask about interest
CTA: Yes, I'm interested / No, remove me
```

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Boring subject line | Provocative, specific, curiosity-driving |
| Slow opening | Value in first sentence |
| Wall of text | Short paragraphs, white space |
| Multiple CTAs | One email, one action |
| Generic copy | Specific to their situation |
| Too long | Match length to email type |
| No preview text | Optimize for mobile |
| Salesy tone | Helpful, valuable, direct |
| Spam trigger words | Clean, professional language |
